# THE PALIMPSEST — Session Guide: The Verso
### A one-night descent for four 5th-level characters · Run alongside the table console (the-verso-table.html)

---

## 0 · THE TRUTH (read this twice, then never show anyone)

Everything below derives from this paragraph. If improvisation is needed mid-session, answer from here and you cannot contradict yourself.

> Years ago, Randy Meisner came to the casino and spent **nine days** in the Verso. At a private table he wagered something enormous and lost — *what* he lost is decided with his player (see §4), but it was heavy enough that the house issued **collateral against the remainder of his account: Trajan**, a remainder constructed from Randy's surrendered memories nine days after his arrival, and assigned to walk beside him as both companion and lien. Before the house took the nine days themselves, Randy did one last thing with his fading knowledge: he recorded a compulsion in his own voice and lodged it with the house, to be transmitted **when the account matured** — a way back in, left by a man who knew he was about to forget the door existed. Last night the account matured. The house recalled its collateral first (Trajan, on watch, walked into the elevator), and the debtor followed (the radio voice — Randy's own voice, distorted — and the failed save). The Concierge was not threatening Randy at the elevator. He was reading the ARRIVALS book, where the entry says *EXPECTED*. The man in the top hat is himself a former patron who settled **by service** and has worked the Cage ever since. Below everything is the **Underwriter** — the *scriptio inferior*, the original text the casino was written over — which every debt in the house is written against, and which the owner upstairs has never met and does not know exists. Every timepiece in the Verso reads **3:17**: the minute the Palimpsest wrote itself over and erased itself from the world. Nothing below the Recto has aged a minute since.

**The rule of rules:** the house can compel *arrival* but is contractually incapable of compelling *choice*. It needs consent for everything that matters. Players can learn this rule, test it, and exploit it. Never break it — it is scarier than mind control and it is the engine of the finale.

---

## 1 · WHAT TONIGHT IS FOR

- Reunite the party (Freight Landing, first third of the night).
- Teach the house's rules through play: consent, debts, true names, the under-writing.
- Reveal that Trajan is *in the ledger* — without revealing what he is. Seed only.
- Put Randy's choice at the Cage in his player's hands, with the whole party watching.
- End at the Stair Down. **Do not let them descend tonight.**

**Session-long stakes (announce through play, not narration):** when the other PCs try to ride the lift to Level 2 with the robot's CD-ROM, the lift accepts the disc, chews on it, and descends to the Verso anyway: *TRANSIT SUSPENDED — PARTY ACCOUNT IN ARREARS.* **Debt outranks permission.** Until Randy's account is settled — by withdrawal, wager, or service — nobody in his party ascends to the vault. The Writs of Transit are now gated behind tonight. The detour is the road.

## 2 · COLD OPEN (read aloud, lights low)

> The room is the color of nothing. No seams. No fixtures. No door — you've checked; you've been checking for a while, though you couldn't say how long, because there is nothing here that ticks.
>
> Trajan stands very still near the far wall. He answers when you speak to him, but one beat too late, like a call on a bad line. When you ask how long he's been here, he thinks about it longer than the question deserves and says, "A while."
>
> Somewhere far away — above you? below? — a slot machine pays out. It sounds like applause.
>
> And then, in the wall that has never had a door in it, there is a door, and it is opening.

The Concierge enters, removes his hat, and says: **"Mr. Meisner. You're earlier than your appointment, but the house is flexible. Will you walk with me, or would you prefer to wait for your party?"**

That last line does three jobs: it tells Randy the house knows the others are coming, it offers a *choice* (rule of rules, demonstrated in the first minute), and it lets you read the table — if Randy walks, run §5 rooms with Randy/Trajan solo and crosscut; if he waits, jump the crosscut forward and reunite early.

**While in the White Room**, available on inspection: the bootprints (Trajan's, entering through a solid wall; none of Randy's at all), the tally of nine scratched at floor level in Randy's handwriting, and — Insight DC 13 on Trajan — his eyes flicking toward the cloakroom wall before anyone knows there's a cloakroom.

## 3 · STRUCTURE & CLOCK (≈3.5–4 hours)

| When | Scene | Console action |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | Cold open — White Room | Verso scene, only White Room revealed |
| 0:25 | **Crosscut A** — upstairs, the other three | Switch to YOUR MAP (casino L1), fog as needed |
| 0:40 | Randy & Trajan: corridor + cloakroom | Reveal Service Corridor, Cloakroom |
| 1:00 | **Crosscut B** — the lift, the disc, the refusal | Back to map; then ding |
| 1:10 | **Freight Landing — ARRIVALS puzzle** | Reveal Freight Landing |
| 1:40 | Full party explores: Lost & Found, Accounts | Reveal as entered |
| 2:25 | **The Cage** — terms, negotiation, the choice | Reveal The Cage |
| 3:05 | Lower Corridor → **The Stair Down** | Reveal Lower Corridor, then Stair |
| 3:20 | Threshold scene, session end | Hold on the Stair |

Crosscut in 10–15 minute beats maximum. If one half of the table goes quiet, cut early. The reveal buttons are in the right panel; the player window updates live.

### Crosscut A — upstairs (the other three wake)
Trajan gone from watch; Randy's bedroll empty, **his boots still beside it**. The radio crackles — but for them the voice doesn't compel (they have no accounts). It *invites*, in a tone like a maître d': "Party of Meisner, your table is ready. The freight elevator is holding." Meanwhile a security sweep (two floors of flashlights and polite goons) makes staying put untenable. Goal of this beat: motion, dread, and the decision to ride.

### Crosscut B — the lift refuses them upward
They have the robot's CD-ROM. Let them feel clever. The tray accepts the disc, the panel glows, the lift hums — and descends. Read: **"TRANSIT SUSPENDED — PARTY ACCOUNT IN ARREARS. THANK YOU FOR YOUR PATIENCE."** Then the doors open a hand's width onto the Freight Landing and stop. Nobody upstairs sold them out; the *building's accounting* did. This is the moment the table learns the Writs run through tonight.

## 4 · THE PLAYER-AUTHORSHIP MOVE (do this before or at session start)

Pull Randy's player aside, or pass a note: *"Two questions, answer privately. What does Randy fear he did before the haunting started? What does he hope he did?"* You are not bound by either answer — but the gap between them is the character, and you should aim the buried memory somewhere in that gap.

**Default if the player gives you nothing:** Randy wagered away the memory of a person — someone who used to sing the lullaby that the corridor muzak almost is. When the muzak resolves for four bars (Corridor clue), hand the player this: *"You know this song. Invent, right now, out loud, the first line of it."* Whatever they say becomes canon and becomes the trailhead of the buried memory.

## 5 · ROOM NOTES (delta over the console cards — checks, prices, traps)

The console cards carry descriptions and clues; this section is the connective tissue.

**Service Corridor.** Mirrors show the under-writing (Investigation DC 12). The wrong-repeating carpet motif is a freebie — anyone stating they study the carpet finds it, no roll. **Klaus's light:** any light spell or Channel Divinity makes under-writing legible in its radius (grant a clue of your choice). Keep a tally: each use adds 1. At a running total of 5, the muzak stops for six full seconds — count them out loud at the table — then resumes. Nothing else happens tonight. (Something noticed. That's all.)

**Cloakroom.** The coat fits Randy perfectly and smells of a campfire he can't place. Ticket stub #0001, decades old, his handwriting. The coat-check log lists Trajan under PROPERTY. Ringing the bell summons the Concierge in 1d4 rounds — he answers questions here as at the Cage (see §7). **The chip stamped MEISNER** (denomination a smudged word) is in the coat pocket — physically hand the player something if you can: a poker chip, a coin, anything.

**Freight Landing — the ARRIVALS puzzle.** Doors won't open for "parties without an appointment." A landing-side character must inscribe each guest in ARRIVALS; the book accepts only **a true thing about the guest, confirmed aloud by that guest through the speaking tube**. One truth → one name → one door-width. No checks required; this is a roleplay engine, not a skill gate. Rulings:
- A trivial truth ("David has a sister") is accepted — the book is not picky, only *honest*. But the house keeps every truth given. Say that line once, flatly, and move on: **"The house thanks you. The house keeps it."**
- A **lie** is accepted too. The door opens. The entry is written in red. Do not explain. (Seed: a red entry is a *debt of correction* — the house may later collect by making the lie true, or by taking the truth it replaced. Next-session material.)
- A **refusal** is honored — rule of rules — but the door stays shut for that person. Let the party solve it socially. If truly stuck, Trajan quietly offers a truth about the refuser ("He grinds his teeth when he's afraid"), which works, and is *much* worse.
- Crunchy alternative (if your table prefers mechanism): brass dial in the car, legend visible only in the landing mirror-plate; relay symbols via tube; three correct opens, each miss dims the car lights one step (three misses: total darkness for ten seconds, then the doors open anyway — the house was only playing).

**Lost & Found.** The CURE claim ticket (David): the Concierge will discuss terms for releasing an unclaimed item to a non-claimant — it costs *a memory of the person you'd use it for.* Quote the terms; let it hang; do not resolve tonight unless David's player pushes hard. **Theft:** pocketing anything unpaid marks the thief (staff smell it). If something is stolen, 1d4+1 **Empty Suits** arrive within ten minutes — politely (stats: Animated Armor, MM p.19, reflavored as headless tailored suits; they bow before initiative). They accept surrender of the item plus "a late fee" (one small memory — the taste of a meal, a name of a stranger) in lieu of violence. The grandfather clock is stopped at 3:17.

**Accounts.** The ledger page: *MEISNER, R. — ACCOUNT OPEN · FLAGGED: EXPECTED*, one gold-redacted deposit, collateral line reading TRAJAN, maturity date last night. **Trajan's entry has a date of MANUFACTURE, nine days after Randy's tally stops.** If Trajan is in the room when this is found, he reads it without expression and says: "That's a clerical error." (It is not. He may even believe it is.) Also present: the owner's old account, marked *SETTLED — BY SERVICE.* Let them connect that to the Concierge — they'll be wrong in an interesting way (it's the *owner* who settled by service; the owner doesn't remember. The Concierge settled the same way. The house has a type.)

**Lower Corridor / Stair.** Save these for last. The corridor's abandoned cart (R. MEISNER — THE USUAL) lands hardest after Accounts. The Stair is the closing image: threshold carving in Randy's hand, verbatim the radio voice's words; the sound of turning pages from below; thirteen steps by count, far more by sight. If Klaus casts light down it, the light comes back up a different color — describe the color and stop talking.

## 6 · THE CONCIERGE (voice & operating manual)

**Voice:** unhurried, courteous, faintly fond — a man who has watched a thousand guests make this same evening's choices and bears none of them ill will. Never sinister on purpose; the sinister is structural. Says "sir" and "madam." Removes his hat indoors. His pocket watch reads 3:17 and he checks it anyway, from habit older than the habit's purpose.

**Hard rules:** he **cannot lie about terms.** He may decline to volunteer information ("That's above my floor, sir" — he means it literally). He cannot compel; he can offer, quote, and wait. If attacked, he does not fight: he picks up his hat, says "I'll give you a moment," and leaves through a wall — and the Empty Suits handle the rest (4 Animated Armors; a hard-ish fight for level 5, and pointless: he reappears at the Cage afterward, unbothered, and adds "damages" to the account).

**Quotables (deal as needed):**
- "The usual table is held, of course."
- Asked who he works for: "The house." Who owns the house? "Above my floor, sir."
- Asked what he was before: a pause; the once-only controlled flinch. "A guest."
- "The house compels no choices, sir. It merely keeps excellent appointments."
- On Trajan, if asked directly: "I'm not at liberty to discuss other accounts. Or their instruments."
- Last line as the party leaves any room: "Mind the carpet."

## 7 · THE CAGE — Randy's choice (the centerpiece)

The Concierge lays it out plainly, because he must. The house holds Randy's deposit: **the nine days, and the thing he wagered away.** The house will return what it holds for exactly one of:

1. **A memory of equal weight, freely offered.** The player names what Randy gives up. The Concierge weighs it honestly: "That is not of equal weight, sir," or "...That will do." Equal weight means *equal* — a defining memory, not a Tuesday. If paid: Randy receives the deposit as a **sealed memory** — a small vellum envelope, warm like skin. It opens when he sleeps. (Run the buried memory as next session's cold open — a playable flashback. Do not improvise its contents tonight; you'll want the week.)
2. **A service.** The house's standing offer: *retrieve one item from the Level 2 vault that the owner upstairs does not know he keeps, and deliver it unopened to the Cage.* This restores transit immediately (the party gets to Level 2 and the Writs!), costs Randy nothing tonight — and means the party goes into the vault working for the thing under the floor. Do not let them clock that last part until they're already committed.
3. **A wager.** The private table. Quick minigame: three draws, each an opposed check — Randy's choice of Charisma (Deception/Persuasion) or Wisdom (Insight) vs. the Concierge's +7. Best of three. Stakes set before the first draw: Randy stakes a named memory per draw lost; the house stakes one-third of the deposit per draw won. Cheating: Sleight of Hand DC 17 — on success it works and *the Concierge notices and allows it*, saying nothing (the house respects a player); on failure the hand is void and the stake is forfeit.
4. **Walk away.** Honored without protest. The account stays open, transit stays suspended — the *service* option remains on the table as the party's only road to the vault, which keeps "is ignorance preferable" alive instead of forcing it.

**Stagecraft:** time the rest of the party's arrival so they *witness* the choice, not learn of it. If they're elsewhere, the Concierge politely waits: "Shall we expect your party first, sir? These things are better witnessed." Even the NPC is on your side about pacing.

**Whatever Randy chooses is correct.** Your job is to make every option cost something true and pay something true.

## 8 · TRAJAN TONIGHT

Play him almost normal — that's the horror. One beat late on answers. Hums the muzak before hearing it. Knows which hallway without being told ("Lucky guess"). If asked directly *what happened at the elevator*: "There was a voice. It sounded like it had the right to ask." If the ledger is found, see §5 Accounts. **Do not confirm what he is tonight.** If a player says it out loud — "Trajan is made of Randy's memories, isn't he" — Trajan should be the one to answer: "That's a strange thing to say about a person," with exactly one beat too long before *person*.

## 9 · CONTINGENCIES

**They go down the Stair anyway.** The first thirteen steps are allowed. At the fourteenth, the sound of turning pages stops — total silence — and the Concierge's voice from the top of the stair, gentle: "The floor below isn't receiving, sir. But your appointment has been noted." The under-writing on the walls rearranges into a single line in Common: **NOT YET.** Any character who continues must make a Wisdom save DC 15 or arrive back at the top of the stair with no memory of turning around (and the house adds the missing minutes to the account — say that out loud). This is a wall made of theme, not hit points.

**They attack the Concierge.** See §6. Fightable suits, unkillable premise.

**They try to leave via the lift without resolving anything.** The lift takes them up to Level 1 freely — *leaving* is always allowed; it's *ascending to Level 2* that's gated. But Trajan cannot board ("RECALLED PROPERTY MAY NOT EXIT" on the panel), and Randy feels the compulsion's hook still set. Walking away from Trajan is a choice the table is allowed to make. Let the silence in the elevator do the work.

**Randy's player is absent.** Don't run this session. Run a Level 1 casino-floor caper instead; the Verso keeps.

**They solve everything fast.** Stretch the Lost & Found (every PC finds one labeled object that *resonates* — improvise a label off their backstory: "the way home," "a brother's face," "three good years"). Or trigger the Empty Suits via the theft seed. Do not start the descent early; end early instead — a short session that ends at the Stair beats a long one that wanders past it.

**Tone check.** Comedy lives in the crosscuts and the puzzle; the Cage and the Stair stay quiet. The moment Clown Fart's player goes silent unprompted, you've won — protect that silence, don't fill it.

## 12 · THE LAST THING TO READ BEFORE PLAYERS ARRIVE

The house compels arrivals, never choices. The Concierge cannot lie about terms. Every clock says 3:17. Trajan is nine days younger than the tally. The book already has a blank line waiting. And whatever Randy decides at the Cage — pay, serve, gamble, or walk — say yes to it, charge full price for it, and end the night standing at the top of a stair that is thirteen steps long and much, much deeper than that.

Mind the carpet.
