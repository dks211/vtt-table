# LEVEL 2 — THE VAULT OF THE BELLA ROSA

## Tonight’s table script and authoritative addendum

This is the quick-running version for the current VTT build. Where it conflicts with the older `level-2-the-vault.md`, **use this document**. The largest changes are:

- Level 2 is reached by the visible stairs below the Verso.
- The landing is followed by **The Unfinished Passage**, then the Mirror Gallery.
- Clown Fart’s performance happens in **Sarlossi’s office** and reveals the vault door.
- Strongbox 0001 contains **Rasura only**. The Concierge keeps the second half of the photograph.
- Touching Rasura restores Randy’s nine days immediately.
- Sarlossi’s sincere renunciation ends the fight.
- There are three blank Writs and one named Writ. The named Writ is also Kalaxia’s prison.

---

# THE TEN-MINUTE PREP

Write these where you can see them:

1. **PAY settles Randy’s debt. A Writ provides passage. They are separate.**
2. **WORK settles only when the named Writ is delivered to the Concierge.**
3. **Rasura gets one cut. It cannot solve two problems.**
4. **Three blank Writs for four PCs. Trajan is not one of the four and cannot leave.**
5. **Do not roll to summon CF’s troupe. They arrive.**
6. **If Sarlossi sincerely renounces the Bella Rosa, the fight ends.**

Recommended Mirror Gallery key: **LEFT — RIGHT — CENTER — LEFT — RIGHT.**

---

# 1. COLD OPEN — THE HOUSE REQUIRES A CHOICE

The decision happens before the party can leave the Verso and descend.

### Read aloud — the Concierge

> “Before you leave this floor, Mr. Meisner’s matured account must be addressed. Not necessarily paid. Addressed.”
>
> The Concierge places two cards on the counter.
>
> “You may **pay**. You surrender every claim to the sealed property held under ticket 0001. The account closes, and you owe the House nothing.”
>
> He touches the second card.
>
> “Or you may **work**. Below us, Don Sarlossi keeps a named document he does not understand that he keeps. Bring that document to me unopened. Upon delivery, the account closes.”
>
> “Payment settles a debt. A Writ permits passage. Kindly do not confuse the two.”

### If they ask whether PAY remains available later

> “The House is indifferent to the hour of payment. If Mr. Meisner reclaims the sealed property before settling, the House will reassess the same weight at that time. Weight is weight.”

Do not identify the contents. This keeps PAY visibly open without spoiling strongbox 0001.

### If they choose WORK

> The Concierge turns the WORK card over. On its back is a single line:
>
> **ONE NAMED DOCUMENT. DELIVER UNOPENED.**
>
> “The account remains open until delivery. You may now descend.”

### If they choose PAY

> The card blackens from the edges inward. Somewhere beneath the floor, a lock opens.
>
> “The account is settled. You may now descend. You will still require valid passage if you intend to leave Val du Cendre.”

### David’s Cure ticket — remind, do not force

> As the Concierge gathers the cards, his eyes settle on David.
>
> “Your Cure ticket remains valid, sir. Belinda will remember you. The remedy will reach her. The price remains every memory you possess of her.”

If David accepts, narrate established images leaving him. Do not ask the player to invent a memory under pressure. No numerical penalty.

# 2. THE STAIRS AND LANDING

### Read aloud

> The stairs below the Verso are ordinary stone stairs. That somehow makes the descent worse.
>
> Halfway down, the casino’s music disappears—not fading with distance, but stopping at a boundary. Your next footfall returns with a new echo.
>
> Painted marble ends at a ruler-straight waterline across the landing. Beyond it, the walls resolve into courses of exposed dressed stone, warm beneath your hand. Crystal fixtures remain above the line, decorative and unwired. Below them, iron sconces hold smoking torches whose light seems much older than the casino.

Pause. Let them move into the landing before revealing the next room.

---

# 3. THE UNFINISHED PASSAGE — THE CLEAN TRANSITION

This room has no puzzle and no encounter. Its purpose is to make the environmental change unmistakable.

### Read aloud

> Beyond the landing is a short chamber the casino never finished disguising.
>
> There is no carpet here. No brass. No mirrors. Only close-set blocks, soot-black mortar, and two iron torches bending in a draft from somewhere below. The ceiling is low enough to touch. Chisel marks continue beneath the floor, as though this passage were cut out of a much larger sentence.
>
> Behind you is a casino. Around you is a dungeon. Ahead, through another stone arch, gold glints again.

If they inspect the masonry:

- The blocks predate modern Val du Cendre.
- Under-writing occupies the mortar joints rather than the stone faces.
- The draft comes from deeper than the visible floor plan permits.

After roughly thirty seconds of table time, reveal the Mirror Gallery.

---

# 4. THE MIRROR GALLERY

### Read aloud

> The raw passage opens into a gallery built from the same ancient blocks, but here the casino has returned in pieces: gilt mirror-plates bolted over old mortar, brass trim stopping wherever the stone refuses a nail, velvet worn thin across flagstones.
>
> At the first junction, three arches wait—left, center, and right. Each looks open from here. In the brass mirror opposite them, two reflections end at blank stone a few feet in. The third keeps going.

### Running it

Use the fixed key:

1. Left
2. Right
3. Center
4. Left
5. Right

No roll is required. Looking in the mirror reveals the continuing passage.

For a wrong arch:

> Three steps in, the passage ends at fitted stone. Dust disturbed by your approach does not settle here—it slides sideways along the floor and disappears through the correct arch.

If they smash indiscriminately through three junctions, the Counting Floor hears them and Sarlossi cannot be surprised. Otherwise, let cleverness be quick.

# 5. THE COUNTING FLOOR

### Read aloud

> Green baize tables stand under hard white lamps. Four employees count chips into regimented towers, calling totals over one another. At your appearance, every hand stops—but no one reaches eagerly for a weapon.
>
> The oldest of them looks from you to the corridor behind you.
>
> “Whatever you’re stealing,” she says, “Sarlossi stole it first.”

### Four viable routes

- Group Stealth DC 12.
- A 50 gp/chip bribe.
- A plausible “Sarlossi sent us” bluff.
- A short fight: three thugs and one veteran.

Do not make the employees suicidal. They surrender, retreat, and accept face-saving explanations.

### Reward for resolving it socially

> “He received three,” the veteran says. “Three Letters of Transit. He expected four. Been sleeping in the vault for two nights, if he sleeps at all. Ask him why and he starts breaking furniture.”

This is the early warning: **three passages for four PCs**.

If the party fights loudly, note: **Sarlossi forewarned; no surprise.**

# 6. SARLOSSI’S OFFICE — CLOWN FART’S SCENE

### Read aloud

> The office is a private music salon built with the appetite of a man who never learned the word enough.
>
> A velvet stage gleams beneath warm footlights. A black grand piano waits beneath a small chandelier. Beside it stands a solitary music stand, its yellowed pages already open.
>
> Sarlossi’s desk occupies the best seat in the room, flanked by an oxblood settee and a private bar. Beyond it is a bank of strongboxes. One brass face is stamped **0001**.
>
> There is no visible vault door.

## The music stand

When CF approaches it:

> The pages are covered in shorthand you recognize as your own—not because you remember writing it, but because your hand already knows what the marks demand.
>
> The first note comes before you decide to play it.

Do not request a roll.

### Return the memories phrase by phrase

Use one beat, then let CF’s player respond before continuing.

> The opening count returns first. You always rushed the fourth beat. They always complained. You always did it anyway.

One troupe member appears and takes a marked position onstage.

> The next phrase gives you the stage marks—not chalk crosses, but people. Where each friend stood. Who leaned into whose light. Who could never enter from the correct side.

More of the troupe arrives, silently.

> Then the failed audition: Sarlossi dismissing you, your troupe turning the rejection into a bit at his expense, everyone laughing on the way out—and Sarlossi’s smile going perfectly still.

Another phrase:

> The little car. The road home. Your foot pressing the brake pedal and finding nothing beneath it. Not resistance. Not failure. Nothing.

The final memory:

> And then the cue you never reached. The exact breath before the finish. The place where the act has remained suspended all these years.

Now the entire troupe is present.

### The finish

> They look to you for the cue.

Let CF describe or roll for the quality of the finish if he wants. The troupe’s presence and the door opening are automatic.

> The last chord lands.
>
> Every performer hits the final mark. For one impossible instant the act is complete.
>
> Then they are gone.

Say nothing evaluative afterward. Let the silence belong to the table.

One performer leaves a battered guitar pick in CF’s hand:

### Plectrum of the Heavenly Host

- Spellcasting focus; bard attunement.
- **1/day:** when casting a bard spell through it, increase that spell’s save DC by 2 for that casting.
- **One More Time, 1/day:** DC 12 Performance as part of casting. Success: cast without spending the slot. Failure: the spell still casts and spends the slot normally.

### Revealing the vault door

> Behind the stage, a seam appears in the paneled wall. Wood folds inward, revealing blank brass, a clock face, and no handle.

The performance reveals the door. **Strongbox 0001 does not.**

# 7. THE OFFICE DISCOVERIES

## Sarlossi’s contract — in the desk, unlocked

Read or hand over this short form:

> **ACCOUNT SETTLED — BY SERVICE**
>
> The House protects Sarlossi’s fortune and forgives his debts.
>
> Sarlossi guards the Vault whenever called.
>
> **Collateral:** memory and recognition of this agreement.
>
> **Release:** surrender the Bella Rosa and every claim upon it.

Sarlossi cannot recognize this record as pertaining to him. Anyone else can.

## Vaedryn’s letter

> Sarlossi—
>
> You possess three passages for four fugitives. Soundgarden is to be the remainder. Keep him alive. Malrick will collect him at dawn. If he takes passage, forward the destination manifest immediately.
>
> —A. Vaedryn

## Strongbox 0001

Ticket 0001 plus Randy’s presence opens it.

> The brass drawer contains no coin and no paper. Only a scrivener’s knife in a fitted black recess.
>
> The handle reads, in Elvish: **RASURA — FOR THE NINTH DAY.**
>
> The blade bears something that is not writing so much as the scar left when writing is removed.

### The moment Randy touches Rasura

Tell Randy directly:

> Nine days return all at once.
>
> You remember this knife fitting your hand. You remember standing in the original scriptorium at 3:17. You remember finding one name written everywhere—in contracts, prayers, blood, architecture—and dragging that name out of the world one record at a time.
>
> The last surviving inscription was filed here because the House accounts for everything.
>
> You remember realizing that anyone who knew what you had done could be made to undo it. So you deposited the knife, sealed the knowledge, and surrendered the nine days containing it.
>
> You are the scrivener. You erased Kalaxia.

He receives this **before** deciding whether those memories will be surrendered as later PAY.

### Rasura

- A +1 dagger and scrivener’s knife.
- It can erase one genuine written record.
- After one cut, it becomes an ordinary dagger.
- The blade bears Kalaxia’s negative scar, not a second positive copy of the name.
- It cuts records, not intentions.

# 8. THE VAULT DOOR

### Read aloud

> The brass door has no handle and no keyhole. Its clock reads 3:17. Above it, in plain Common:
>
> **THE VAULT OPENS WHEN THE HOUR IS TRUE.**

The answer is CF’s running watch. Accept roughly correct positioning.

> When the hands match the watch, both timepieces tick forward once together. Somewhere inside the wall, a lock remembers how to open.

# 9. DON SARLOSSI AND THE VAULT

Switch to tactical view.

### Read aloud

> The vault is older than every room behind you: bare dressed stone beneath the casino’s gold. Coin dunes climb around four pillars. A chandelier hangs from a black counterweight chain. Brass reservoirs line one wall; a shallow map-drawer occupies another.
>
> Don Sarlossi stands in the center at two in the morning. He looks as though he has only just noticed he is here.
>
> “You,” he says, with genuine exhaustion. “Do you have any idea what I have spent to keep people out of this room?”

## If they show or read the contract

> Sarlossi’s eyes pass over the page once without catching. Then someone reads his own name aloud.
>
> His hand tightens around his cane.
>
> “No,” he says. Then, more quietly: “How long?”

Let them persuade him. The decisive question is not whether he believes the paper; it is whether he can surrender the Bella Rosa.

### Sincere renunciation — the fight ends

If he truly says he gives up the casino and every claim upon it:

> Sarlossi looks past you, through the stone, toward the casino above.
>
> “Then let it have the house,” he says. “I have mistaken a sentence for a kingdom long enough.”
>
> Something enormous shifts behind his eyes—and finds no guardian there.
>
> The gold leaf beginning at his throat flakes away. His cane strikes the floor once. He does not raise it again.

No fight. The House is neutral; the release term has been fulfilled.

### They reach him, but the Persuasion fails

He fights, but the revelation shakes the transformation. When Phase 2 begins, **Molten-Leaf Breath starts expended and must recharge**.

> “I did not build forty years of my life,” he says, “to surrender them because a piece of paper claims I already did.”

### They never reach him

He turns at full strength, breath available.

## Phase 1 — Sarlossi

- AC 15; HP 60.
- Two cane attacks: +6, 1d8+4.
- He tries to take Klaus alive.
- Transform at half HP or when someone reaches for the Writ drawer.

## The transformation

> Gold leaf crawls up Sarlossi’s throat like frost. For one second, the man and the thing the House made of him remember one another.
>
> His eyes find Clown Fart.
>
> “The little car,” he says. A wet, golden laugh catches in his throat. “That was you? You weren’t supposed to walk away from that.”

Optional, if Trajan is standing plainly before him:

> Sarlossi’s gaze shifts. “And you. I paid for you.”
>
> Trajan does not look away. “You paid the House.”

Then transform.

## Phase 2 — Vault Guardian

- AC 17; HP 110.
- Bite +7, 2d10+5.
- Claw +7, 2d6+5.
- Molten-Leaf Breath: 40-foot line, DC 14 Dex, 10d6 fire.
- Scouring Breath: 30-foot cone, DC 14 Con; failure removes reactions and bonus actions until the end of the target’s next turn.

## Initiative 20 — add the lair marker when combat starts

Rotate:

1. Coin spray / difficult terrain.
2. Collapse a pillar; rubble becomes difficult terrain.
3. Persistent molten-leaf hazard.
4. Telegraph a floor-unwrite zone for one round, then remove it.

## Trajan’s first intervention — no roll

When the first breath would seriously threaten the party:

> The dragon’s throat fills with liquid gold.
>
> Trajan is already moving. One clean cut takes the chandelier’s counterweight chain. Brass and crystal crash between the dragon and the party, ruining the breath before it leaves its mouth.
>
> He is very good at cutting the thing that makes the machine crash.
>
> He has done it before.

The breath is wasted. Afterward, Trajan may **Make Amends** once when a PC would fall to 0: move them clear and take the effect himself.

# 10. THE FOUR WRITS

### Read aloud

> The map-drawer contains four shallow trays.
>
> Three hold Writs with blank name-lines.
>
> The fourth is already filled. The marks are impossible to read, but Randy can match their shape to the negative scar on Rasura’s blade.
>
> There are three empty passages for four of you. The fourth passage already has a passenger.

If they do not understand:

> Trajan looks from the blade to the Writ. “The name and the cage are the same line.”

If they still need it stated plainly, use the Concierge’s voice through the House:

> “Kalaxia’s name is written in exactly one place in all the world. There. Scrape it away and the Writ becomes blank. You will also remove the only pin holding him outside reality.”

The instant anyone touches the named Writ:

> Every stopped clock in the building chimes once. Clown Fart’s watch skips a beat.

## Put these outcomes openly on the table

### Return the intact named Writ

- WORK completes only upon delivery to the Concierge.
- Randy’s debt then settles and locked transit restores.
- Kalaxia rides out as the named passenger.
- The party still has only three blank Writs: one PC stays.

### Erase Kalaxia’s name and write the fourth PC’s name

- All four PCs receive passage.
- Kalaxia is released into the world.
- The named document was not returned; Randy’s debt remains open.
- Rasura is spent.

### Keep the named Writ intact

- Kalaxia remains filed outside reality.
- Randy’s debt remains open.
- Only three blank passages are available: one PC stays for now.

### PAY later

- Randy surrenders the restored nine days and unused Rasura.
- His debt settles.
- Kalaxia remains filed.
- This does **not** create a fourth Writ. One PC still lacks passage.

The House cannot be tricked. A valid Writ permits passage anywhere—even out of the Verso with debt unpaid—but it carries only its named passenger.

### The line to remember

> One cut. It cannot solve two problems.

# 11. WHO STAYS — CONSEQUENCES

- **Klaus leaves:** the destination manifest goes to Vaedryn. Pursuit waits at the other end.
- **Klaus stays:** Malrick arrives at dawn. Do not decide the result tonight.
- **Randy stays:** Accounts has a desk with a fresh brass nameplate: R. MEISNER.
- **David stays:** make the result depend on whether he redeemed the Cure and whether he remembers Belinda.
- **Clown Fart stays:** Trajan remains with him. Trajan says, sincerely: “This is the beginning of a beautiful friendship.”

Trajan is recalled property and cannot use a Writ to leave. He is not counted among the four PCs competing for three blank passages.

# 12. RETURN TO THE CONCIERGE

If they deliver the named Writ:

> The Concierge accepts it without opening it.
>
> Somewhere inside the casino, a lock releases—not a door, but a condition.
>
> “Work completed,” he says. “Account settled.”

He gives Randy the second half of the old photograph. Together the halves show Randy, Rynard, and the younger Concierge. The back reads:

> **DON’T / LET THEM WAKE IT**

Do not clarify whether “it” means Kalaxia or something deeper beneath the Palimpsest.

Finally, ask CF’s player:

> “What time does your watch show?”

- If the named Writ was delivered or the name erased: **3:18**.
- If the name remains filed and nothing resolves: **3:17**.

End on the player’s answer.
